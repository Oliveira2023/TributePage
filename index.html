Dr. Norman Borlaug

The man who saved a billion lives

Here's a time line of Dr. Borlaug's life:

  • 1914 - Born in Cresco, Iowa
  • 1933 - Leaves his family's farm to attend the University of Minnesota, thanks to a Depression era program known as the "National Youth Administration"
  • 1935 - Has to stop school and save up more money. Works in the Civilian Conservation Corps and the US Forestry Service
  • 1937 - Finishes university and takes a job in the US Forestry Service
  • 1938 - Marries wife Norma Stiles
  • 1941 - Tries to get ahead by asking questions in school instead of answering them
  • 1942 - Receives Nobel Peace Prize
  • 1945 - Rejects a 100% salary increase from his job and takes a job in the US Forestry Service
  • 1946 - Marries wife Norma Stiles
  • 1952 - Tries to get ahead by asking questions in school instead of answering them
  • 1961 - Receives a Ph.D. in Genetics
  • 1962 - Rejects a 100% salary increase from his job and takes a job in the US Forestry Service
  • 1973 - Finishes university and takes a job in the US Forestry Service
  • 1981 - Marries wife Norma Stiles
  • 1983 - Tries to get ahead by asking questions in school instead of answering them
  • 1984 - Receives a Ph.D. in Genetics
  • 2005 - Rejects a 100% salary increase from his job and takes a job in the US Forestry Service
  • 2009 - Retires and starts a new job as a US Forest Service Forest Ranger
  • 2012 - Marries Norma Stiles
